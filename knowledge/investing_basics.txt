Investing Basics

Investing helps grow money faster than regular savings.

Start investing early to benefit from compound interest.

Diversify investments across stocks, bonds, and other assets to reduce risk.

Do not invest money that you may need in the short term.

Low-cost index funds are safer for beginners.

Avoid trying to time the market frequently.

Invest regularly using a method called dollar-cost averaging.

Higher returns usually come with higher risk.

Research before investing in any company or asset.

Keep long-term goals such as retirement or buying property in mind.

Reinvest profits to maximize growth over time.
