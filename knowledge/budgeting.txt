Personal Budgeting Guide

The 50/30/20 rule is a simple budgeting method:
50% of income for needs (rent, food, utilities),
30% for wants (entertainment, shopping),
20% for savings and investments.

Always track expenses weekly or monthly.
Use categories such as rent, groceries, transport, bills, and entertainment.

Pay fixed expenses first before spending on wants.

Create a monthly spending limit for each category.
Do not exceed your planned budget.

Review your budget at the end of every month and adjust based on actual spending.

Avoid impulse purchases. Wait 24 hours before buying non-essential items.

Automate savings so money moves to savings before you spend.

Budgeting helps reduce debt and increases financial stability.
